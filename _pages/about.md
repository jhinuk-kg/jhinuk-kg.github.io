About
======
I'm an operations and coordination professional based in Downtown Toronto, drawn to mission-driven organizations — nonprofits, education, and community-serving teams — where the work is human-scale and the outcomes are measured in the people served, not the dashboards refreshed.

Most of what I do lives quietly in the background: a steady hand on day-to-day operations, so nothing slips through the cracks when everyone's heads-down on the work that matters; lighter lift for program staff and educators, so their energy goes where it should; documentation that actually serves the organization — for funders, audits, institutional memory, and the long haul; and lean systems for scheduling, communication, and tracking that are built with care, explained clearly, and maintained consistently.

From my time at CAIDP, I bring a particular lens to responsible and safe AI-assisted workflows — where thoughtful, human-first technology deployment is central to everything. The goal is always the same: free up humans for the human work, so the mission gets more of the care and attention it deserves.

Background
======
Research-based M.A. in Sociology at Queen's University, Kingston, with a focus on mental health and courtesy stigma. Prior roles include project manager at QMIND (Queen's University) supervising research on AI ethics and policy, research internships on diaspora, transnationalism, and AI governance, and content writing for Gradifying and Sociology Group at the intersection of sociology, policy, and technology.
